Course Overview

The psychology course provides a comprehensive exploration of human behavior, cognition, and mental processes. Participants will gain insights into various psychological theories, research methodologies, and practical applications in different contexts.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand foundational theories and principles in psychology.
  • Explore research methodologies and experimental design in psychology.
  • Examine psychological disorders and therapeutic interventions.
  • Analyze the influence of culture and environment on behavior.
  • Apply psychological concepts to real-world scenarios and case studies.

Outline

This week's material covers foundational concepts in psychology, including:

  • Overview of psychology as a discipline.
  • Major psychological perspectives: psychoanalytic, behaviorist, cognitive, humanistic.
  • Research methods in psychology: experimental, observational, survey, case study.
  • Biological bases of behavior: brain structure, neurotransmitters, and neural pathways.
  • Developmental psychology: theories of cognitive, social, and emotional development.

Week 2: Psychological Disorders and Therapies

  • Week 2 Content (PDF)
  • Week 2 Quiz

Outline

This week's material explores psychological disorders and therapeutic approaches:

  • Classification and diagnosis of psychological disorders: DSM-5 criteria.
  • Common psychological disorders: anxiety, mood, psychotic, and personality disorders.
  • Biopsychosocial model of mental illness: integrating biological, psychological, and social factors.
  • Therapeutic interventions: psychotherapy, behavior therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT).
  • Psychopharmacology: medications used in the treatment of psychological disorders.
  • Ethical considerations in psychological research and practice.
